Mask Disposal

In light of the COVID-19 Pandemic, the usage of face masks has been increased all over the world, with a number of countries mandating it for indoor and outdoor public settings. The implementation of masks all around the world for everyday use has had a detrimental effect on our environment, as well as wildlife. It is no secret that littering can smother environments and breakup ecosystems. It can also affect our wildlife, as animals can mistake pieces of fabric for prey, increasing the risk of them choking, or consuming unhealthy elements. Just one mask can produce millions of particles as it decomposes which can carry chemicals and bacteria up the food chain and potentially into humans.

Guidelines to Mask Disposal

  • 1If available, it is encouraged to use a 3-ply reusable mask or N-95 reusable mask. Although we understand this is not always the case.
  • 2After taking off your disposable mask, cut or rip off, one side of the string so that there is no longer a loop, make sure the other side stays attached to the mask. This reduces the choking hazard for animals.
  • 3Place the mask in your general garbage bin at home, or a general garbage bin at school. Make sure that the lid is closed on the bin, especially if it is outdoors! Do not recycle, recycling masks clogs machinery and puts workers at risk!

In the UK a picture was taken by a BBC News photographer of a mask stuck in the legs of a seagull. For more information on the BBC News, please click on the image above.
